Numerical Integration

Calculate area under the curve with precision

What is Numerical Integration?

Numerical integration constitutes a broad family of algorithms for calculating the numerical value of a definite integral. It is used when an analytical solution is difficult or impossible to find.

  • Function f(x): The mathematical equation of the curve you want to find the area under (e.g., x^2 + sin(x)).
  • Bounds (a & b): The interval [a, b] on the x-axis. You can use math constants like pi, 2*pi, or e.
  • Trapezoidal Rule: Area ≈ (h / 2) * [f(x_0) + 2*Σf(x_i) + f(x_n)]
  • Simpson's 1/3 Rule: Area ≈ (h / 3) * [f(x_0) + 4*Σf(x_odd) + 2*Σf(x_even) + f(x_n)]
    * Note: Requires an EVEN number of subintervals (n).
  • Simpson's 3/8 Rule: Area ≈ (3h / 8) * [f(x_0) + 3*f(x_1) + 3*f(x_2) + 2*f(x_3) + ... + f(x_n)]
    * Note: Requires the number of subintervals (n) to be a MULTIPLE OF 3.
