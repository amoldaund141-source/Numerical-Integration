Numerical Integration

Calculate area under the curve with precision

What is Numerical Integration?

Numerical integration constitutes a broad family of algorithms for calculating the numerical value of a definite integral. It is used when an analytical solution is difficult or impossible to find.

  • Function f(x): The mathematical equation of the curve you want to find the area under (e.g., x^2 + sin(x)).
  • Bounds (a & b): The interval [a, b] on the x-axis over which you are integrating.
  • Subintervals (n): The number of segments the interval is divided into. Higher numbers generally yield higher accuracy.
  • Trapezoidal Formula: Area ≈ (h / 2) * [f(x_0) + 2*Σf(x_i) + f(x_n)]
  • Simpson's 1/3 Formula: Area ≈ (h / 3) * [f(x_0) + 4*Σf(x_odd) + 2*Σf(x_even) + f(x_n)]
    * Note: This rule strictly requires an even number of subintervals (n).
